V-Dem v15 (2024) + SIPRI Arms Transfers (2025)

Politicide by Alignment

Do regimes aligned with the West show more restraint in political violence than those aligned with Russia and China? Exploring arms trade alignment and state repression (1950–2024).

Read the backstory
In 2018, Simon Roth and Fabio Votta stumbled upon a tweet with a pretty bold claim:
Regimes aligned with the West are more likely to restrain themselves when it comes to repressive measures, while countries aligned with Russia are more likely to commit violence against their own population because they don't have to fear losing their support.

We couldn't agree more that political science makes such claims testable. And so a challenge was born: find out what the data actually says.

The first big question: how do you measure alignment over time? We scraped the military trade register from SIPRI and used arms transfers as a proxy. The more a major power exports weapons to a country, the more likely that buyer is aligned with the seller.

It holds up well. Pre-1979 Iran shows up as Western-aligned with massive US arms flows. After the revolution, it flips to Russia/China-aligned. Egypt switches after Camp David. Cuba flips in 1960.

Now we've rebuilt from scratch with V-Dem v15 (2024) and SIPRI data through 2025, using a 5-year rolling window with category-weighted trade counts and stricter thresholds.

Methodology

Data Sources

V-Dem v15 Varieties of Democracy (2024). 8 repression measures: political killings, torture, physical violence, civil liberties, rule of law, media censorship, journalist harassment, freedom of expression.

SIPRI Arms Transfers Database (through 2025). 29,917 trade records from Western countries and Russia/Soviet Union/China.

Alignment Classification

Uses a 5-year rolling window (year−2 to year+2) with category-weighted trade counts. Major platforms (aircraft, ships, armored vehicles, air defense) count 3×, missiles and artillery count 2×, sensors and engines count 1×.

A country-year is Western-aligned if ≥70% of weighted trades come from the West, or Russia/China-aligned if ≥70% come from Russia/China. Minimum 5 raw trades in the 5-year window required. The map uses a continuous score: (w_west − w_russia) / w_total. Time series use a 3-year rolling average for smoothness.
